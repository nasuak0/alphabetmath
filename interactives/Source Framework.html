Framework → Page Effect

How leaning on the five sources as a framework shapes your interactives — without touching the Zmath design. Each source feeds a different layer: two decide scope, two decide presentation, one is a reusable format.

02

The difficulty ladder you inherit

one strand, two altitudes + a bridge
FLOOR

Foundations

IM Grade 8

Concrete, hands-first. Whole numbers, draggable figures, one idea at a time.

BRIDGE

Core

G8 → pre-university

Variables both sides, brackets, real coordinates. Formal language starts here.

CEILING

Pure

Sadler & Reese

Fractional & literal equations, trig, conics, the run-up to calculus.

Effect on the page: tiles can carry a level badge, and the home menu reads as a climb rather than a flat shelf — which suits the console-tile metaphor.

04

One voice, every page

Better Explained's reveal order, made a rule
1

Hook

Open on one concrete thing the visitor can grab — a point to drag, a shape to slide.

2

Visual

An immediate payoff appears as they act. The idea is seen before it's named.

3

Name it

Only then attach the formal term, notation, or theorem — now it has something to stick to.

This is already your house line — "Stop memorizing — start visualizing." The framework just makes it a consistent shape every blurb and opening frame follows.

05

The one guardrail

what keeps the page publishable

Scaffolding, not source material

Use all five for what to teach, how to sequence, and how to phrase the intuition — but the figures, wording, and problems on the page must be original. Only IM is openly licensed; the rest are not.
